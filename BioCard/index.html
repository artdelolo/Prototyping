ABOUT LOLO

Lorena “Lolo” Lopez is a very tired and sleepy graduate student who took on way too many projects that all somehow happened to be due this week. But she’s still trying to have a positive outlook and just make it through the next two days. Aside from her design and museum work though she has another cool talent not many people have seen, so she’s sharing it with you all today. Lorena’s undergraduate degree is in Fine Art, concentrating in Film Photography, and she take super neat photos on vintage cameras around the world.




PROCESS

While a lack of access to a dark room has slowed down Lorena’s ability to produce photographs, she still tries her hardest to make as many pictures as she can. When she travels for leisure, instead of taking usual family photographs, she only carries around a 1957 Yashica-Mat medium format camera with which to catalog her adventures. Is it purely aesthetic? Perhaps. But everywhere the camera goes people strike conversations with her to learn more, so it opens many doors for new friends around the world. The negatives are then developes in a film lab in Ohio and shipped back to Miami where Lorena scans each of them individually.



This series of photographs from my latest tours through England. These photographs are from Spain, Switzerland and England.
